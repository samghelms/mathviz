§27.7 Lambert Series as Generating Functions

Notes:
See Apostol (1990, pp. 24–25).
Keywords:
Lambert series, number theory, number-theoretic functions

Lambert series have the form

27.7.1 ∑n=1∞f⁡(n)⁢xn1-xn.
Symbols:
n: positive integer and x: real number

If |x|<1, then the quotient xn/(1-xn) is the sum of a geometric series, and when the series (27.7.1) converges absolutely it can be rearranged as a power series:

27.7.2 ∑n=1∞f⁡(n)⁢xn1-xn=∑n=1∞∑d|nf⁡(d)⁢xn.
Symbols:
d: positive integer, n: positive integer and x: real number
