On the circle of convergence, |z|=1, the Gauss series:

  • (a)

    Converges absolutely when ℜ⁡(c-a-b)>0.

  • (b)

    Converges conditionally when -1<ℜ⁡(c-a-b)≤0 and z=1 is excluded.

  • (c)

    Diverges when ℜ⁡(c-a-b)≤-1.

For the case z=1 see also §15.4(ii).

The principal branch of F⁡(a,b;c;z) is an entire function of a, b, and c. The same is true of other branches, provided that z=0, 1, and ∞ are excluded. As a multivalued function of z, F⁡(a,b;c;z) is analytic everywhere except for possible branch points at z=0, 1, and ∞. The same properties hold for F⁡(a,b;c;z), except that as a function of c, F⁡(a,b;c;z) in general has poles at c=0,-1,-2,….

Because of the analytic properties with respect to a, b, and c, it is usually legitimate to take limits in formulas involving functions that are undefined for certain values of the parameters.

For example, when a=-m, m=0,1,2,…, and c≠0,-1,-2,…, F⁡(a,b;c;z) is a polynomial:

15.2.4 F⁡(-m,b;c;z)=∑n=0m(-m)n⁢(b)n(c)n⁢n!⁢zn=∑n=0m(-1)n⁢(mn)⁢(b)n(c)n⁢zn.
