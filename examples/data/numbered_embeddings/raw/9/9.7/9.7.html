§9.7(iii) Error Bounds for Real Variables

Notes:
See Olver (1997b, pp. 392–393 and 413–414).

Permalink:

In (9.7.5) and (9.7.6) the nth error term, that is, the error on truncating the expansion at n terms, is bounded in magnitude by the first neglected term and has the same sign, provided that the following term is of opposite sign, that is, if n≥0 for (9.7.5) and n≥1 for (9.7.6).

In (9.7.7) and (9.7.8) the nth error term is bounded in magnitude by the first neglected term multiplied by 2⁢χ⁡(n)⁢exp⁡(σ⁢π/(72⁢ζ)) where σ=5 for (9.7.7) and σ=7 for (9.7.8), provided that n≥1 in both cases.

In (9.7.9)–(9.7.12) the nth error term in each infinite series is bounded in magnitude by the first neglected term and has the same sign, provided that the following term in the series is of opposite sign.
