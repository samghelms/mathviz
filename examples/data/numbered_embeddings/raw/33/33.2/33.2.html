This differential equation has a regular singularity at ρ=0 with indices ℓ+1 and -ℓ, and an irregular singularity of rank 1 at ρ=∞ (§§2.7(i), 2.7(ii)). There are two turning points, that is, points at which d2w/dρ2=0 (§2.8(i)). The outer one is given by

The choice of ambiguous signs in (33.2.3) and (33.2.4) is immaterial, provided that either all upper signs are taken, or all lower signs are taken. This is a consequence of Kummer’s transformation (§13.2(vii)).

Fℓ⁡(η,ρ) is a real and analytic function of ρ on the open interval 0<ρ<∞, and also an analytic function of η when -∞<η<∞.

The normalizing constant Cℓ⁡(η) is always positive, and has the alternative form

As in the case of Fℓ⁡(η,ρ), the solutions Hℓ±⁡(η,ρ) and Gℓ⁡(η,ρ) are analytic functions of ρ when 0<ρ<∞. Also, e∓i⁢σℓ⁡(η)⁢Hℓ±⁡(η,ρ) are analytic functions of η when -∞<η<∞.
